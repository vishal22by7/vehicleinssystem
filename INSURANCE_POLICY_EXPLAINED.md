## Understanding Insurance in Simple Terms

Think of insurance like a **safety net**. You pay a small amount (premium) every year, and if something bad happens to your vehicle, the insurance company helps pay for repairs or replacement.

## üìã Types of Insurance Policies

## üí∞ Important Terms You Must Know

### **IDV (Insured Declared Value)**
**What it is:** The maximum amount insurance will pay if your vehicle is **completely destroyed** (total loss).

**How it's calculated:**
- For new car: 95% of showroom price
- For 1-year-old: 85% of showroom price
- For 2-year-old: 75% of showroom price
- And so on... (decreases with age)

**Example:**
- Car showroom price: ‚Çπ10,00,000
- Car is 2 years old
- IDV = ‚Çπ7,50,000 (75% of ‚Çπ10,00,000)
- **If car is completely destroyed, you get ‚Çπ7,50,000**

---

### **Premium**
**What it is:** The money you pay every year for insurance.

**Example:**
- Premium: ‚Çπ12,000 per year
- You pay this to keep insurance active

---

### **Depreciation**
**What it is:** The value your vehicle loses as it gets older.

**How it affects claims:**
- New parts cost ‚Çπ50,000
- But your car is 3 years old
- Insurance pays: ‚Çπ50,000 - depreciation (maybe ‚Çπ10,000) = ‚Çπ40,000
- You pay the difference: ‚Çπ10,000

**Zero Depreciation Add-on:**
- If you buy this add-on, insurance pays full amount (no depreciation deduction)
- Costs extra: 15-20% more premium

## üö® What Happens When Vehicle is Completely Damaged? (Total Loss)

### **Scenario 1: Total Loss - You GET Money! üí∞**

**When it happens:**
- Car is completely destroyed (cannot be repaired)
- Repair cost > 75% of IDV
- Car is stolen and not recovered
- Car is burned completely

**What you get:**
- **IDV amount** (the insured declared value)
- **NOT ‚Çπ0!** You get the IDV amount

**Example:**
- Your car IDV: ‚Çπ7,50,000
- Car is completely destroyed in accident
- **You get: ‚Çπ7,50,000** ‚úÖ
- You can buy a new car with this money

**Important:** This is ONLY if you have **OD or Comprehensive** policy. TP policy won't pay for your vehicle.

---

### **Scenario 2: Total Loss - But Claim is Rejected ‚ùå**

**When you get ‚Çπ0:**
- Policy expired (not active)
- You were driving drunk
- You don't have valid license
- Damage happened outside policy period
- Fraud detected

**Example:**
- Car is completely destroyed
- But your policy expired last month
- **You get: ‚Çπ0** ‚ùå
- You lose everything

---

### **Scenario 3: Partial Damage - You Get Repair Money üíµ**

**When it happens:**
- Car is damaged but repairable
- Repair cost < 75% of IDV

**What you get:**
- **Repair cost** (minus depreciation, if applicable)
- **NOT the full IDV**

**Example:**
- Car IDV: ‚Çπ7,50,000
- Accident damage repair cost: ‚Çπ2,00,000
- **You get: ‚Çπ2,00,000** (for repairs) ‚úÖ
- Car is repaired, you keep using it

## üíµ How Much Money You Get (Payout Calculation)

## ‚ùì Common Questions

### **Q1: If my car is completely destroyed, do I get ‚Çπ0?**
**A:** NO! You get the **IDV amount** (could be ‚Çπ5-10 lakhs). This is the maximum payout.

### **Q2: Why do I get less money than repair cost?**
**A:** Insurance payout is based on premium and severity, not actual repair cost. This is standard practice. You might pay some difference.

### **Q3: What if repair cost is more than IDV?**
**A:** If repair cost > 75% of IDV, it's declared "total loss" and you get full IDV amount (not repair cost).

### **Q4: Can I claim for normal wear and tear?**
**A:** No. Insurance covers accidents/damage, not normal wear (old battery, worn tires, etc.).

### **Q5: What if I'm at fault in accident?**
**A:** Still covered! Comprehensive policy covers your fault too.

### **Q6: What if someone else is at fault?**
**A:** You can claim from your insurance OR from their insurance. Your choice.

### **Q7: How long does claim take?**
**A:** 
- Auto-approved: Hours
- Manual review: 1-3 days
- Total loss: 7-30 days (needs surveyor)
